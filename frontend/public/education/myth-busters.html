STI Myths & Facts

Let's debunk common misconceptions about STIs with accurate, science-based information.

MYTH:

"You can tell if someone has an STI by looking at them."

FACT:

Many STIs show no visible symptoms. The only way to know for sure is through testing. Regular testing is essential for sexually active individuals.

MYTH:

"STIs only affect certain types of people or lifestyles."

FACT:

STIs can affect anyone who is sexually active, regardless of age, gender, sexual orientation, or relationship status. Protection is important for everyone.

MYTH:

"Birth control pills protect against STIs."

FACT:

Birth control pills only prevent pregnancy. They provide no protection against STIs. Barrier methods like condoms are needed for STI prevention.

MYTH:

"Once you've had an STI and it's treated, you can't get it again."

FACT:

You can get most STIs multiple times, even after treatment. Previous infection doesn't make you immune. Continued protection is necessary.

MYTH:

"If you have no symptoms, you don't have an STI."

FACT:

Many STIs can be asymptomatic (show no symptoms) for long periods. Regular testing is crucial even if you feel healthy.

MYTH:

"STIs always cause obvious symptoms quickly."

FACT:

Some STIs may not show symptoms for weeks, months, or even years. Some people never develop noticeable symptoms but can still transmit the infection.

Important Information

Remember

  • Regular testing is essential for sexual health
  • Use protection consistently
  • Open communication with partners is crucial

Take Action

  • Get tested if you're sexually active
  • Learn about prevention methods
  • Seek medical advice when needed
