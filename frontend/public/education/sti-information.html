Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

Understanding STIs is crucial for maintaining sexual health. Learn about common infections, their symptoms, transmission, and treatment.

HIV/AIDS

Symptoms:

  • Fever and night sweats
  • Muscle aches and fatigue
  • Sore throat and headache
  • Swollen lymph nodes

Transmission:

  • Unprotected sexual contact
  • Sharing needles
  • Mother to child during pregnancy

Treatment:

Managed with antiretroviral therapy (ART)

Chlamydia

Symptoms:

  • Abnormal discharge
  • Burning during urination
  • Pain in lower abdomen
  • Often no symptoms

Transmission:

  • Unprotected sexual contact
  • Mother to baby during childbirth

Treatment:

Curable with antibiotics

Gonorrhea

Symptoms:

  • Green/yellow discharge
  • Pain during urination
  • Swollen genitals
  • Rectal pain or discharge

Transmission:

  • Unprotected sexual contact
  • Mother to baby during childbirth

Treatment:

Treated with specific antibiotics

Syphilis

Symptoms:

  • Painless sores
  • Skin rash
  • Fever and fatigue
  • Progresses in stages

Transmission:

  • Direct contact with sores
  • Mother to baby during pregnancy

Treatment:

Penicillin injections

HPV

Symptoms:

  • Genital warts
  • Often no visible symptoms
  • Can lead to certain cancers

Prevention:

  • Vaccination available
  • Regular screening

Treatment:

Treatment depends on symptoms

Important Information

Key Facts

  • Many STIs show no symptoms initially
  • Regular testing is essential for sexually active individuals
  • Early detection leads to better treatment outcomes
  • Most STIs are curable or manageable with treatment

When to Seek Care

  • Unusual discharge or bleeding
  • Pain during urination or sex
  • Sores, bumps, or rashes
  • After potential exposure
